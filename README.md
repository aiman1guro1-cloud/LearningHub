Online Learning Management System (LMS)

An Online Learning Management System (LMS) is a web-based platform designed to support the creation, management, delivery, and tracking of educational courses and training programs over the internet. It enables instructors and administrators to organize and distribute learning materials, while allowing students to access content, complete activities, and monitor their academic progress anytime and anywhere.

The system provides a digital learning environment where instructors can upload lectures, videos, documents, and assessments. Students can log in to view course materials, submit assignments, participate in discussions, and track their performance.

It also includes essential tools for managing courses, users, assessments, communication, and analytics. Administrators oversee system-wide operations such as user and course management, instructors handle course content and student evaluation, and students engage with the learning materials and collaborate through discussions.

This type of system is widely used in educational institutions, training centers, and organizations to enhance accessibility, flexibility, and efficiency in both academic and professional learning.

Core Features of the LMS
  User Management
  Course Management
  Enrollment System
  Progress Tracking
  Communication Tools
  Reports and Analytics

Access and Permissions

Students can only access the materials of courses they are enrolled in. They can participate in discussion forums by posting and commenting, and can view instructor information. Instructors can also engage in discussions and are responsible for creating, updating, and managing their own learning materials.
